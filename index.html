So what causes them?

Our earth's crust is divided into what we call "tectonic plates". These plates move at a very slow pace, and sometimes will slip over, past or underneath each another, releasing stress. This release causes the ground underneath us to shake, sometimes forming earthquakes. British Columbia happens to be near the boundary of two of these plates, the North American Plate, and the Juan de Fuca Plate, putting us in the line of fire.

BC is a seismically active region where over 3,000 small quakes are recorded each year. In the past 300 years, we’ve had 5 quakes between the magnitudes of 7.3 and 9.0. Earthquakes of this magnitude result in large tsunamis, cause major destruction and result in many casualties. Scientists believe there’s a 37% chance the ‘Big One’, another major subduction quake, will occur in the next 50 years.
