So what causes them?


Our earth's crust is divided into what we call "tectonic plates". These plates move at a very slow pace, and sometimes will slip over, past or underneath each another, releasing stress. This release causes the ground underneath us to shake, sometimes forming earthquakes. British Columbia happens to be near the boundary of two of these plates, the North American Plate, and the Juan de Fuca Plate, putting us in the line of fire.

BC is a seismically active region where over 3,000 small quakes are recorded each year. In the past 300 years, we’ve had 5 quakes between the magnitudes of 7.3 and 9.0. Earthquakes of this magnitude result in large tsunamis, cause major destruction and result in many casualties. Scientists believe there’s a 37% chance the ‘Big One’, another major subduction quake, will occur in the next 50 years.

Prepare your household.

Make a family plan
  • Make a master list of family members, photos of each member, and emergency numbers and put it in a document.
  • Print this document to put in your emergency kit or keep on your phone.
  • Keep in mind that local phone calls might be impossible if an earthquake affects cell towers - include phone numbers of family from out of town and ask that person to be your emergency contact.

Decide on a meeting place
  • Decide on where you will meet in advance. Select two meeting places where you and your family can get back together if you get separated: 1) one close to your home, and 2) one that is a little further away. Some common options are a community centre, school or library.
  • For families with young kids, identify teachers, family friends or other contacts that might be located closer to your child in an emergency.

Prepare your household
  • Identify objects in your home that might be toppable or dangerous during an earthquake and secure them to the wall or a stable object.
  • Make sure everyone knows where your electrical panel, fire extinguisher, and water and gas valves are located and how to use them or turn them off.
  • Plan evacuation routes from each room in your home, making sure no one is isolated from the rest of your family.

Build an emergency kit.

One of the best ways to get your family or business prepared for a natural disaster event is to prepare ‘grab-and go-bags’. By gathering and organizing your emergency supplies now, you can be ready when disaster strikes.
